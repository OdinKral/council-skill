1. **A Unix Manifesto for the Age of AI** — arguing that taste (sustained refusal of complexity) is the only safeguard against entropy as AI removes friction

### Copy-1: "The Bayesian Branch" — Formalize Decision Heuristics

We build an explicit utility function for compute allocation. A lightweight decision gate: `decision_value = info_gain × reversibility_cost × blast_radius`.

**Month 2:** Feels great. We stop invoking full councils for obvious decisions. The gate starts generating data about itself.

**Month 6:** Coherent resource model. Every expensive operation has an expected value.

**Risk:** Over-formalization becomes its own complexity. The gate must be simpler than the decisions it governs.

**Probability of good outcome:** 65%

### Copy-2: "The Deletion Branch" — Aggressive Simplification

Treat every existing component as debt until proven otherwise. Audit, cull, simplify.

**Month 2:** The system breathes. 9 sharp tools instead of 15. Context windows less cluttered.

**Month 4:** The thinking gets clearer too. Removing unused components eliminates cognitive option cost.

**Month 6:** Field deliverables ship. Weekly reviews take 20 minutes instead of 40.

**Risk:** Premature culling of quietly load-bearing components.

**Probability of good outcome:** 70%

### Copy-3: "The Intuition Branch" — Status Quo

Continue with intuition-driven allocation. The system works, but it doesn't compound. No dashboard. Slow complexity drift.

**Probability of good outcome:** 55%
